Vardhaman College of Engineering (VCE), located in Shamshabad, Hyderabad, is a well-regarded autonomous institution. Students generally praise its strong placements, clean infrastructure, and solid academics. However, the campus is known for being strictly regimented, featuring a strict phone ban and a somewhat remote location.A breakdown of the student and alumni feedback across key areas:PlacementsHigh Success Rate: Placements are a strong suit, particularly for CSE and IT branches, with companies like Accenture, Capgemini, Cognizant, and TCS visiting regularly.Packages: The average salary package generally ranges around $4,000 to $6,000 per annum, while peak packages have reached up to $32,000.Support: Many students appreciate the Placement and Training (PAT) cell, which actively prepares them for corporate drives.Academics & FacultyRigorous Academics: The curriculum is updated and industry-focused. The college is autonomous (though formerly JNTUH affiliated), helping it maintain highly structured exam patterns.Teaching Quality: Faculty members are highly qualified, with many holding doctoral degrees. Most students find them supportive, though a few feel the teaching can sometimes lean too heavily toward rote learning rather than practical industry exposure.Infrastructure & Campus LifeFacilities: The campus is spacious, green, and well-maintained. It features modern labs, Wi-Fi-enabled smart classrooms, and a large central library.Sports & Extracurriculars: The campus boasts great sports facilities, including a well-maintained cricket turf. However, some students note that cultural fests and fests, in general, are less frequent because the college prioritizes academics.Drawbacks & Pain PointsStrict Rules: On platforms like Reddit, current students mention strict regulations, including a firm ban on mobile phones inside the campus, which many find restrictive.Location: Situated in Shamshabad, the campus is quite a distance from the city center. The long commute can be exhausting, and some students point out that relying on college transport can get expensive.Hostel & Transport Fees: According to feedback across Glassdoor, several students find the college bus and hostel fees to be on the higher side.
